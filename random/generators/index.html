Generators for Pairs of Position-Orientations

In our group, we frequently work with data on position-orientation space \(\mathbb{M}_d := \mathbb{R}^d \times S^{d - 1}\). Position-orientation space \(\mathbb{M}_d\) is naturally acted on by the roto-translation group \(\operatorname{SE}(d)\): \[ g := (y, R) \in \operatorname{SE}(d), p := (x, n) \in \mathbb{M}_d \mapsto g p := (y + R x, R n). \] It can therefore be desirable to perform processing on \(\mathbb{M}_d\) that is equivariant to \(\operatorname{SE}(d)\), which essentially means that roto-translations commute with the processing.

A particularly interesting situation is when we want to process point clouds of position-orientations. Suppose we are looking for equivariant functions (e.g. neural networks) \(\Phi: \mathbb{M}_d \to \mathbb{R}\). It is not so hard to see that this permits only constant functions \(\Phi\): since \(\mathbb{M}_d\) is a homogeneous space of \(\operatorname{SE}(d)\), for any \(p_1, p_2 \in \mathbb{M}_d\), there exists a \(g \in \operatorname{SE}(d)\) such that \(g p_1 = p_2\), so necessarily \(\Phi(p_1) = \Phi(p_2)\).

Since this is rather restrictive, we now look instead for equivariant functions \(\Phi: \mathbb{M}_d \times \mathbb{M}_d \to \mathbb{R}\). Fortunately, there are actually non-trivial equivariant scalar functions on pairs of position-orientations. In Roto-Translation Invariant Metrics on Position-Orienation Space, Bellaard & Smets classify a whole family of such functions in the case \(d = 3\). These functions are the lengths of specific exponential curves connecting pairs of position-orientations: we get different functions by varying the parameters of the \(\operatorname{SE}(3)\)-invariant metric. An exponential curve \(\eta: [0, 1] \to \mathbb{M}_3\) \[ \eta(t) := \exp(t M) p \] for some position-orientation \(p \in \mathbb{M}_3\) and generator \(M \in \mathfrak{se}(3)\); its length can easily be computed using \[ L_\mathcal{G}(\eta) = \|M p\|_\mathcal{G}, \] where \(\mathcal{G}\) is the \(\operatorname{SE}(3)\)-invariant metric. It turns out that for any pair \(p_1, p_2 \in \mathbb{M}_3\) there exist many generators \(M(p_1, p_2)\) such that \(\eta(0) = p_1\) and \(\eta(1) = p_2\), with \(\eta(t) := \exp(t M(p_1, p_2)) p_1\).

In Roto-Translation Invariant Metrics on Position-Orienation Space, Bellaard & Smets pick the generator with the minimal angular velocity \(M_\mathrm{mav}\). This corresponds to the exponential curve which rotates the least. Another interesting choice is the pure rotation generator \(M_\mathrm{rot}\), which connects the pair by only rotating around some point. Generically, the collection of all possible connecting generators is diffeomorphic to the circle \(S^1\). It is therefore possible to parametrise all such generators using an angle \(\phi \in [-\pi, \pi)\), where by convention \(M_0 := M_\mathrm{mav}\).

In this visualisation, we show a pair of position-orientations \(p_1 := (x_1, n_1), p_2 := (x_2, n_2) \in \mathbb{M}_3\) in black, and three connecting exponential curves: \[ \begin{aligned} \textcolor[rgb]{0.7137254901960784, 0.19607843137254902, 0.10980392156862745}{\eta_\mathrm{mav}(t)} & := \exp(t M_\mathrm{mav}(p_1, p_2)) p_1, \\ \textcolor[rgb]{0, 0.6078431372549019, 0.3333333333333333}{\eta_\mathrm{rot}(t)} & := \exp(t M_\mathrm{rot}(p_1, p_2)) p_1, \\ \textcolor[rgb]{0, 0.44313725490196076, 0.7372549019607844}{\eta_\phi(t)} & := \exp(t M_\phi(p_1, p_2)) p_1. \end{aligned} \]
