Diffusion-Shock Filtering on the Space of Positions and Orientations

Finn Sherry, Kristina Schaefer, Remco Duits

arXiv

We extend Regularised Diffusion-Shock (RDS) filtering from two dimensional Euclidean space to the space of positions and orientations. This has numerous advantages, e.g. making it possible to enhance and inpaint crossing structures, since they become disentangled when lifted. We create a version of the algorithm using gauge frames to mitigate issues caused by lifting to a finite number of orientations. This leads us to study generalisations of diffusion, since the gauge frame diffusion is not generated by the Laplace-Beltrami operator. RDS filtering compares favourably to existing techniques such as Total Roto-Translational Variation flow , Non-Local Means , and BM3D when denoising images with crossing structures, particularly if they are segmented. Additionally, we see that RDS inpainting on the space of positions and orientations is indeed able to restore crossing structures, unlike its Euclidean counterpart.
